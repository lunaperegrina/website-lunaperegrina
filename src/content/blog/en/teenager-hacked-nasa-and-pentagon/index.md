It sounds made up, but it is real.

A **15-year-old teenager managed to break into systems from NASA and the U.S. Department of Defense**.

His name was **Jonathan James**, known online by the handle **c0mrade**.

This happened in the late 1990s and became one of the most famous cases in hacking history.

## Who was Jonathan James

Jonathan James was an American teenager deeply interested in computing and security.

From a very early age, he started to:

- program
- explore systems
- study networks
- test vulnerabilities

He was exactly the type of person who spends hours trying to understand **how systems work and how to break them**.

According to reports from the time, he was already coding and had familiarity with languages such as **C** while still very young.

## Early incidents

Before drawing attention from federal authorities, Jonathan James had already breached some smaller systems.

One of the earliest known cases involved **a telecommunications company** in the United States.

He gained access to the company systems, but apparently **did not cause significant damage**, so the incident did not have major consequences at that moment.

But the situation would change quickly.

## The NASA intrusion

In 1999, Jonathan James gained access to computers belonging to **NASA**.

According to later investigations, he broke into systems connected to the **Marshall Space Flight Center**, which was responsible for projects related to the International Space Station.

During the intrusion, he:

- accessed internal data
- copied software used in space station systems
- exploited multiple vulnerabilities in servers

NASA later estimated the incident caused around **$41,000 in costs** to investigate and recover affected systems.

## The Department of Defense intrusion

In addition to NASA, Jonathan James also accessed systems belonging to the **U.S. Department of Defense**.

He exploited an existing **backdoor in a compromised system**, which allowed him to enter the network and collect different kinds of internal data.

Among the accessed information were:

- internal messages
- sensitive documents
- network data

At that point, the case drew the attention of the **FBI**.

## The investigation

After tracing the activity back to Jonathan James, federal agents executed a search warrant at his home.

He was only **15 years old** at the time.

The case drew major media attention because it was the **first time a minor was convicted of hacking crimes in the United States**.

According to reports, when agents arrived at his house, he made an ironic comment that this was one of the most memorable days of his life.

## The sentence

Because he was a minor, Jonathan James received a relatively lenient sentence.

He was sentenced to:

- **house arrest**
- **probation**
- **a ban on using computers**

The computer restriction was a central part of the sentence.

For someone deeply involved with technology, that was practically equivalent to losing his main hobby.

## Later consequences

Years later, another major hacking case involving government data emerged in the United States.

Although Jonathan James was **not formally charged in that case**, he came under investigation again.

The continuous pressure from investigations and public attention had a significant impact on his life.

## Why this story became famous

The Jonathan James case became emblematic for several reasons:

- he was only **15 years old**
- he accessed extremely sensitive systems
- the case exposed serious security weaknesses
- it showed how vulnerabilities can be exploited by isolated individuals

It also marked an important moment in the history of **cybersecurity**, when governments started treating cyber threats much more seriously.

Cases like this helped shape how **digital security is handled today**.
